We are delighted to extend a warm welcome to you at the Grand Egyptian Museum.

Situated in the heart of the capital, the Egyptian Museum stands as one of the largest and most renowned international museums.

Its origins trace back to 1958 when it found its first home in Azbakeya Park, a location rich with a diverse array of antiquities at that time.

Distinguished as one of the world's earliest public museums, the Egyptian Museum houses an impressive collection of over 180,000 artifacts. Among its most significant holdings are the archaeological treasures unearthed in the tombs of kings and the royal entourage during the Middle Dynasty in Dahshur in 1894.

The museum has evolved over the years to become home to the world's largest collection of archaeological sites, offering a comprehensive portrayal of every stage in ancient Egyptian history. We look forward to sharing this rich cultural heritage with you during your visit.
